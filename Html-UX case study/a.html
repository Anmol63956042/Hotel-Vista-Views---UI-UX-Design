Project Overview

Hotel Vista Views is a luxury hotel website concept built to highlight services (Restaurant, Spa, Staycation), present an elegant brand identity, and make it effortless for visitors to find contact & reservation information. The UI was designed using Canva and refined into this UX deliverable.

Problem Statement

Many hotel websites overwhelm visitors with clutter and unclear navigation. This project aims to deliver a clean, engaging, and trustworthy experience that guides users from discovery to contact/update.

Design Goals

  • Create a luxury, cohesive visual language
  • Clear navigation: Homepage → Services → Contact
  • Highlight services with high-quality imagery
  • Design for quick access to contact and reservations

Information Architecture & User Journey

The site follows a simple linear IA for clarity:

  1. Homepage — brand hero, visual introduction
  2. Services — Restaurant, Spa, Staycation (cards + descriptions)
  3. Contact — address, phone, email, social

User journey: Discover → Explore services → Contact (reservation) — designed to reduce friction and emphasize the hotel's premium experience.

Typography

Heading example — elegant serif
Body example — clean sans-serif for readability. Use serif for headings to convey luxury and sans-serif for long-form copy.

Imagery & Brand

Photography is central to the brand — use warm, well-lit images of rooms, amenities and experiences. Maintain a consistent crop and aspect ratio for service cards to preserve visual rhythm.

Usage recommendations:
  • Hero images: 1600×900 or larger for crisp presentation
  • Service card images: consistent square crop (1:1)
  • Use subtle overlays when placing text over images to ensure legibility

Usability & Recommendations

The current design is visually strong. To improve overall UX and conversions, consider:

  • Add a persistent booking call-to-action (top-right) that opens a simplified booking modal or contact form
  • Implement responsive adjustments for small screens (stack service cards, enlarge CTA)
  • Include microcopy around booking policies, cancellation & parking info to reduce friction
  • Collect user feedback and A/B test headline CTAs to optimize booking rates

Conclusion

Hotel Vista Views presents a polished, luxury-first design that balances aesthetics with usability. With a few interaction improvements (booking flow, responsiveness and customer testimonials), this design can become a high-converting marketing & booking platform for the hotel.

Next steps: prototype booking flow, develop responsive components, and run a usability test with 5–8 participants to validate assumptions.
